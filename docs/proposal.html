Project Overview

Summary

Working on top of this cloud simulation done by Sebastian Lague with Unity, we will add simulations of lightning, rain, and god rays through atmospheric rendering.

Problem Description

We want to develop a realistic simulation of different weathers. Problems we are trying to solve include: how to simulate lightning, atmospheric volumetric light scattering (god rays), and how to make the clouds roll naturally. To implement these effects, we are going to refer to other tutorials, articles, and research papers.

Goals and Deliverables

Baseline Deliverables

  • Currently we already have a real-time cloud simulator in Unity. Our goal is to add real-time lightning to the project. We will deliver an interactive real-time rendering that simulates lightning in Unity. In particular, we will make a video that demonstrates this happening.
  • To simulate a lightning storm, we will have lightnings which start from clouds and end on the ground. The lightnings should have a tree-like structure and make its way from a point in the sky to a point on the terrain below.
Performance Metrics
  • How realistic is the lightning color compared to real images
  • How well does the lightning branch out relative to actual lightning
  • Frames Per Seconds in Unity on NVIDIA GForce GTX 1660 (Best graphics card of our group)

Aspirational Deliverables

  • Non-random spawning of lightning in places of high cloud density with terrain sampling.
  • Atmospheric rendering of rain during storms
  • God rays generation after the a storm ends has ended.
  • [Stretch Goal] Clouds that move according to fluid dynamics and roll along terrain and in the sky rather than just move and shift around.
